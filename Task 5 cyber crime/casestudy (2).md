# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
UPI / Online Payment Fraud (a form of Financial Cyber Fraud)

---

## Case Study

**Title:** The "Wrong Transfer" Trap — A UPI Fraud Targeting College Students

Ravi, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, posted on a college Facebook group that he was selling his old laptop for ₹15,000. Within hours, he received a call from someone who introduced himself as "Arjun from Indore," claiming to be interested in buying the device.

Arjun said he would send payment via UPI and asked Ravi to share his UPI ID. Moments later, Ravi received a UPI notification on his phone. Arjun then told Ravi: "I accidentally sent ₹15,000 instead of ₹1,500 — please return ₹13,500 immediately or my boss will terminate me." Feeling pressured and sympathetic, Ravi quickly transferred ₹13,500 back. He then checked his account and realised the original "incoming" notification was a fake UPI request — Arjun had sent a collect/payment request disguised to look like a credit. No money had ever arrived. Ravi had sent ₹13,500 to a fraudster with no way to recover it.

**Who is targeted:** Students selling items online, first-time UPI users, and individuals unfamiliar with the difference between a payment received and a payment request.

**Consequences:** Financial loss (often unrecoverable), emotional distress, and damaged trust in digital payment systems. In many cases, the fraudster is untraceable.

**Source:** Based on common UPI fraud patterns documented by NPCI and reported by Cyberdost (cyberdost.gov.in).
