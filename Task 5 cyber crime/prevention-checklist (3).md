# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
## (For College Students in India)

---

### General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every online account. A strong password has at least 8 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on your email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is compromised, 2FA provides a second layer of protection.

3. **Never click on unknown links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, email, or social media. These may be phishing links designed to steal your login credentials or install malware.

4. **Verify before you trust.** If someone online claims to be from your bank, NPCI, or any government body and asks for sensitive information — hang up or ignore the message and call the official helpline directly.

5. **Keep your software and apps updated.** Security patches in updates fix known vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.

6. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities** such as internet banking, logging into college portals, or accessing personal email. Use mobile data or a VPN instead.

---

### Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Understand the difference between "Send Money" and "Request Money" on UPI apps.** A scammer may send you a collect request that looks like an incoming payment notification. You are the one paying — not receiving — when you enter your UPI PIN.

8. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, customer support, or government agency. No legitimate organisation will ever ask for this information.

---

### Reporting Cybercrime in India

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24x7, toll-free)
- **Cyberdost (Awareness):** @Cyberdost on Twitter/X

> If you are a victim of online fraud, call **1930 immediately** to freeze the fraudulent transaction before the money is withdrawn. Time is critical.
