# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com)

## Design Description
The infographic is titled **"Digital Literacy: Your Superpower in the Digital Age"** and is designed as a single A4 portrait slide with a clean, modern layout in blue and white tones. It is divided into three visual sections:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – A short definition block explaining that digital literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, and create information using digital technologies. An icon of a laptop with a lightbulb represents this concept.

2. **Safe Internet Practices** – A checklist-style panel with icons covering: using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal data online.

3. **Professional Online Presence** – A 3-step infographic showing how students can build their digital identity: setting up a LinkedIn profile, creating a GitHub account, and maintaining a professional tone on social media.

## Reflections
The most interesting aspect of this task was realising how much a single well-designed visual can communicate compared to a paragraph of text. I found it challenging to decide which information to include versus what to leave out — digital literacy is such a broad topic. I chose Canva because it provided free templates suitable for student-level infographic design without requiring any design experience.
