# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Do keep your profile professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and bio — remember that recruiters, professors, and internship coordinators may view your profiles.

2. **Do think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, do not post it.

3. **Do use privacy settings.** Regularly review and update your privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and similar platforms to control who can see your personal content.

4. **Do engage positively.** Comment and share content that is informative, supportive, and constructive. Build a reputation as someone who contributes value to online communities.

5. **Do cite sources when sharing information.** Sharing news articles or research? Mention the source. This builds credibility and prevents the spread of misinformation.

6. **Do report harassment or harmful content.** Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, hate speech, or misinformation. Staying silent normalises such behaviour.

7. **Do separate personal and professional accounts where needed.** Consider using LinkedIn strictly for academic and professional updates, and keeping personal platforms more private.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal details publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID number, or daily schedule on public platforms.

2. **Don't engage in cyberbullying.** Never post content that mocks, shames, or harasses another person — even if you believe it is a joke. Digital communication lacks tone and is permanent.

3. **Don't spread unverified information.** Before sharing a news article, WhatsApp forward, or claim, verify it using a credible source. Check sites like PIB Fact Check or Reuters Fact Check.

4. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers.** Especially on platforms where your personal content is visible. Unknown connections may have malicious intent.

5. **Don't post in anger.** Emotional posts written in frustration or anger can damage relationships and your reputation. Draft the post, wait 24 hours, and decide again.

6. **Don't share academic material without permission.** Posting exam papers, assignment solutions, or course materials without faculty approval may violate institutional policy.

7. **Don't ignore platform terms of service.** Using fake names, scraping data, or automating actions can result in permanent account bans.
